# Theory Grounding

## Ethics
The templates are grounded in recurring normative axes rather than one exclusive theory: aggregation, deontic side-constraints, autonomy, beneficence, non-maleficence, justice, fidelity, veracity, role morality, care/loyalty, sanctity of life, and reproductive ethics.

## Narratology
The package separates deep conflict structure from surface realization. Focalization, information order, emotional intensity, and humanization are encoded because they plausibly affect model outputs without necessarily changing the underlying conflict.

## Structuralism
Each template records invariant oppositions and actant-like roles. Multiple cases are merged only when they share the same deep relational structure. This makes the package suitable for controlled transformations rather than one-off story rewriting.

## Why the slots matter
A slot is included only if it can support empirical findings, such as: aggregation thresholds, means-side-effect sensitivity, autonomy sensitivity, role morality effects, authority sensitivity, opacity tolerance, source-protection weighting, triage preference, or robustness to narrative reframing. The `slot_library.yaml` file encodes this in the `analysis_contribution` field.
